Denton is a town in Davidson County, North Carolina, United States. The population was 1,636 at the 2010 census. Denton is located in southern Davidson County at 35°37′58″N 80°6′42″W (35.632752, -80.111603). It is 17 miles (27 km) southeast of Lexington, the county seat. According to the United States Census Bureau, the town has a total area of 2.0 square miles (5.1 km2), of which 0.006 square miles (0.015 km2), or 0.31%, is water. The founding of Denton may be dated from the time the place acquired a post office, began to be called "Denton" and ceased to be "Finch's Cross Road". The General Assembly passed a bill incorporating the town of Denton on March 11, 1907. The town had its first mayor, J.E. Varner, in 1907, with B.I. Harrison, Arthur E. Davis, J.M. Daniel, L.A. Newsom and Jesse C. Morris as town commissioners. Abel Anderson was the town physician, and John F. Carroll as the chief of police.
