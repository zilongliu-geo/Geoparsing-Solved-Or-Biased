Durham is a town in Androscoggin County, Maine, United States. The population was 3,848 at the 2010 census. It is included in both the Lewiston-Auburn, Maine Metropolitan Statistical Area and the Lewiston-Auburn, Maine Metropolitan New England City and Town Area. Durham was named after County Durham, England, the ancestral home of the town's first settler. According to the United States Census Bureau, the town has a total area of 39.04 square miles (101.11 km2), of which 38.28 square miles (99.14 km2) is land and 0.76 square miles (1.97 km2) is water. Durham is bordered by Pownal, Auburn, Freeport, New Gloucester, Brunswick, Lewiston, Lisbon Falls, and Topsham. Durham is in the Maine's 2nd US Congressional District, Maine Senate District 15, and Maine House of Representatives District 105. Durham Community School is a public school operated by the Durham School Department. The school provides for the education of students in grades K through 8.
