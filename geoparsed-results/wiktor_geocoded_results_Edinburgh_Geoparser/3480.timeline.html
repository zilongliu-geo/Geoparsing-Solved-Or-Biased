Hudson is a city in Angelina County, Texas, United States. The population was 4,731 at the 2010 census. Hudson is located in central Angelina County at 31°19′48″N 94°47′50″W (31.330062, -94.797360), adjacent to the western border of Lufkin, the county seat. Texas State Highway 94 passes through the community, leading southwest 30 miles (48 km) to Groveton. According to the United States Census Bureau, Hudson has a total area of 5.1 square miles (13.2 km2), of which 5.1 square miles (13.1 km2) is land and 0.04 square miles (0.1 km2), or 0.76%, is water. As of the census of 2000, there were 3,792 people, 1,288 households, and 1,013 families residing in the city. The population density was 827.9 people per square mile (319.7/km²). There were 1,390 housing units at an average density of 303.5/sq mi (117.2/km²). The racial makeup of the city was 83.91% White, 3.96% African American, 0.21% Native American, 0.29% Asian, 9.81% from other races, and 1.82% from two or more races. Hispanic or Latino of any race were 17.06% of the population.
