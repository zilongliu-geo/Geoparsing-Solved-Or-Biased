Alma is a town in Ellis County, Texas, United States. The population was 331 at the 2010 census. Alma is located on Interstate 45 about 5 miles (8.0 km) southeast of Ennis. According to the United States Census Bureau, the town has a total area of 5.0 square miles (13 km2), all of it land. As of the census of 2000, there were 302 people, 107 households, and 83 families residing in the town. The population density was 60.0 people per square mile (23.2/km²). There were 114 housing units at an average density of 22.7 per square mile (8.8/km²). The racial makeup of the town was 91.06% White, 3.64% African American, 5.30% from other races. Hispanic or Latino of any race were 5.96% of the population. There were 107 households out of which 37.4% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 67.3% were married couples living together, 8.4% had a female householder with no husband present, and 21.5% were non-families. 19.6% of all households were made up of individuals and 7.5% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.82 and the average family size was 3.23.
