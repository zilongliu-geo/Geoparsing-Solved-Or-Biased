Easton is a city in Buchanan County, Missouri, United States. The population was 234 at the 2010 census. It is part of the St. Joseph, MO–KS Metropolitan Statistical Area. Easton was platted in 1854. Easton is located at 39°43′22″N 94°38′23″W (39.722644, -94.639783). According to the United States Census Bureau, the city has a total area of 0.58 square miles (1.50 km2), all of it land. State Highway 31 runs North/South through the center of town and is used primarily as a link between US Hwy 36 to the North and US Hwy 169 to the South. The speed limit is enforced aggressively on Hwy 31 in the same fashion as Macks Creek, Missouri and Randolph, Missouri for a major source of the small community's income. Easton is the birthplace of legendary college basketball coach Henry Iba, who coached Oklahoma State (then known as Oklahoma A&M) to consecutive NCAA titles in 1945 and 1946, becoming the first two-time winning coach in the NCAA tournament. Easton is the home of D-II All-American runner, and the 2004 Missouri NCAA Woman of the year, Kate Hamera.
