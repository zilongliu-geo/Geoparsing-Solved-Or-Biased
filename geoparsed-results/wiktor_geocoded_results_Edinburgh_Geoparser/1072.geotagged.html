Franklin is a home rule-class city in and the county seat of Simpson County, Kentucky, United States. The population was 8,408 at the 2010 census. Franklin was formally incorporated by the state assembly on November 2, 1820, on a 62-acre (250,000 m2) tract of land. This tract was purchased from William Hudspeth and was named for Benjamin Franklin. The post office was established on September 29, 1822, with Robert W. Simpson as postmaster. Franklin is located at 36°43′21″N 86°34′39″W (36.722487, -86.577566). According to the United States Census Bureau, the city has a total area of 7.4 square miles (19 km2), all land.
