Santo Tomas is a fifth class and smallest municipality in the province of Pangasinan, Philippines. According to the 2010 census, it has a population of 14,406 people. According to the 2007 Philippine general election, Santo Tomas has a voting population of 6,652 voters. It has a land area of 1,429 hectares. The town's principal products are palay, yellow corn, coconut, tobacco, poultry and livestock. Santo Tomas is politically subdivided into 10 barangays.
