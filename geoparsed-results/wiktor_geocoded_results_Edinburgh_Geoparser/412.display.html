Salem is a city in and the county seat of Marion County, Illinois, United States. The population was 7,485 at the 2010 census. Salem is located at 38°38′N 88°57′W (38.6282, -88.9482). According to the 2010 census, Salem has a total area of 7.097 square miles (18.38 km2), of which 6.94 square miles (17.97 km2) (or 97.79%) is land and 0.157 square miles (0.41 km2) (or 2.21%) is water. Public schools: Salem Community High School Salem Elementary School Selmaville Elementary School Catholic schools: St. Theresa of Avila William Jennings Bryan, 41st US Secretary of State; US congressman representing Nebraska's 1st district; born in Salem Charles W. Bryan, 20th and 23rd governor of Nebraska; younger brother of William Jennings Bryan; born in Salem Jim Finks, pro football player and executive; attended high school in Salem Merle Harmon, sports broadcaster; born in Salem Jess Marlow, television journalist; born in Salem Morrie Steevens, pitcher for the Chicago Cubs and Philadelphia Phillies; born in Salem. Salem is home to Miracle Whip salad dressing and three homes on the National Register of Historic Places: the Charles and Naomi Bachmann House, the Badollet House, and the William Jennings Bryan Boyhood Home.
