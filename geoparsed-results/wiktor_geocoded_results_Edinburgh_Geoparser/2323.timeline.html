Dayton is an unincorporated community and census designated place (CDP) located within South Brunswick Township, in Middlesex County, New Jersey, United States. At the 2010 United States Census, the CDP's population was 7,063. Dayton has a post office with its own ZIP code (08810) that encompasses the entire CDP, as well as some surrounding areas. Dayton is located at 40°22′52″N 74°30′48″W (40.381014,-74.513355). According to the United States Census Bureau, the CDP had a total area of 2.102 square miles (5.443 km2), of which, 2.099 square miles (5.436 km2) of it was land and 0.003 square miles (0.007 km2) of it (0.13%) was water. At the 2000 census, there were 6,235 people, 2,061 households and 1,690 families residing in the CDP. The population density was 1,130.2/km² (2,929.2/mi²). There were 2,252 housing units at an average density of 408.2/km² (1,058.0/mi²). The racial makeup of the CDP was 61.54% White, 9.90% African American, 0.22% Native American, 25.04% Asian, 0.05% Pacific Islander, 1.30% from other races, and 1.96% from two or more races. Hispanic or Latino of any race were 5.15% of the population.
