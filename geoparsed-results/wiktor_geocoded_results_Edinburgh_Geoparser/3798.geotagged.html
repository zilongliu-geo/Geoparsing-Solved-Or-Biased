Corralitos is a census-designated place (CDP) in Santa Cruz County, California, United States. The population was 2,326 at the 2010 census. Corralitos is located at 36°59′29″N 121°48′7″W (36.991303, -121.802013). It is located on Corralitos Creek, a tributary of Salsipuedes Creek which is in turn the lowest tributary of the Pajaro River. According to the United States Census Bureau, the CDP has a total area of 9.0 square miles (23 km2), of which, 9.0 square miles (23 km2) of it is land and 0.16% is water. The 2010 United States Census reported that Corralitos had a population of 2,326. The population density was 258.4 people per square mile (99.8/km²). The racial makeup of Corralitos was 1,980 (85.1%) White, 16 (0.7%) African American, 12 (0.5%) Native American, 48 (2.1%) Asian, 190 (8.2%) from other races, and 79 (3.4%) from two or more races. Hispanic or Latino of any race were 532 persons (22.9%).
