Plymouth is a town in Chenango County, New York, United States. The population was 1,804 at the 2010 census. It is an interior town in the northern part of the county. The town is northwest of the city of Norwich. Families began to settle Plymouth around 1794. The town was formed from the town of Norwich in 1806. According to the United States Census Bureau, Plymouth has a total area of 42.4 square miles (109.7 km2), of which 42.2 square miles (109.2 km2) is land and 0.15 square miles (0.4 km2), or 0.40%, is water. New York State Route 23 is an east-west highway through the town. Kirk – A hamlet, located on NY-23, southwest of Plymouth village and north of Plymouth Reservoir. Plymouth – The hamlet of Plymouth is at the junction of County Roads 16 and 21. The community was originally called "Frinkville". Plymouth Reservoir – A reservoir in the southwest part of the town. Sherburne Four Corners – A hamlet located in the northeast corner of Plymouth on County Road 20, partly in other towns of Chenango County.
