Kanawha River cleanup planned Saturday. CHARLESTON, W.Va. (AP) -- Volunteers are being sought to help clean up the banks of the Kanawha River in Charleston. The event is being held from 8:30 a.m. to 12:30 p.m. Saturday from the 35th Street Bridge to Haddad Riverfront Park. Volunteers are being asked to check in between 7:45 a.m. and 8:30 a.m. along the river near the state Capitol steps on Kanawha Boulevard. Duties include removing debris, sidewalk edging and mulching the riverbank. Breakfast, lunch and work gloves will be provided. In case of rain, the makeup date is April 11. The event is being sponsored by Generation Charleston in conjunction with the West Virginia Make it Shine program.
