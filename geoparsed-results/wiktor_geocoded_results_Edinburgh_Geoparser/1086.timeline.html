Franklin is an unincorporated community and census-designated place in Crawford County, Kansas, United States. As of the 2010 census it had a population of 375. Franklin is located along U.S. Route 69 1 mile (1.6 km) south of Arma. Franklin has a post office with ZIP code 66735. Franklin began as a mining community in the early 1900s. It is located just off Highway 69 Bypass which is a major corridor between Kansas City and Pittsburg, Ks./Joplin, Mo. Franklin was a shipping point on the Joplin & Pittsburg electric railroad. The first post office in Franklin was established in 1908. On May 4, 2003, a high-end F4 tornado ripped through Franklin, the path reached over 0.5 miles (0.80 km) wide at points. Franklin was all but destroyed, the U.S. Post Office, community center, and approximately 1/3 of family homes were destroyed. Four deaths and approximately 20 injuries were reported.
