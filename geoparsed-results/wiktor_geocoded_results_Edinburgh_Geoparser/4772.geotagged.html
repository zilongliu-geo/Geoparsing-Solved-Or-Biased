Venice is a city in Sarasota County, Florida, United States. Venice consists of a large section of main land, as well as Venice Island just off the coast. The city is located south of Nokomis, Florida and north of Englewood, Florida. As of the 2010 census, the city had a population of 20,748. It is noted for its large snowbird population and was voted as top 10 Happiest Seaside Towns by Coastal Living. Venice is a principal city of the Bradenton-Sarasota-Venice, Florida Metropolitan Statistical Area. In the 1870s, Richard Roberts established a homestead near Roberts Bay. In 1884, he sold a portion of his holdings to Frank Higel. Higel established a citrus operation involving the production of several lines of canned citrus items, and for the next 30 years the Higel family members were boat builders, fishermen, grove caretakers and contractors. Darwin Curry was the first postmaster. The Higel and Curry families chose the name "Venice" for their community post office, located south of Shakett Creek on what is now Portia Street in the unincorporated community of Nokomis.
