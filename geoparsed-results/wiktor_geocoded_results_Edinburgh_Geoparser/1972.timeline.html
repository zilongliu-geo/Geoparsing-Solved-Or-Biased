Bridgewater is a town in Grafton County, New Hampshire, United States. The population was 1,083 at the 2010 census. The land comprising Bridgewater was originally part of a town known as New Chester, which was separated into Bridgewater, Bristol, Hill, Danbury, and Wilmot. The town name was chosen because many of the settlers were from Bridgewater, Massachusetts. Bridgewater originally included all of Bridgewater Hill and Bristol. It was first settled in 1776, by Thomas Crawford. It was incorporated 12 February 1788. In 1874, the Bridgewater station was served by the Montreal railroad. According to the United States Census Bureau, the town has a total area of 21.5 square miles (56 km2), of which 21.3 sq mi (55 km2) is land and 0.2 sq mi (0.52 km2) is water, comprising 0.84% of the town. The highest point in Bridgewater is Peaked Hill, at 1,905 feet (581 m) above sea level. Bridgewater lies fully within the Merrimack River watershed.
