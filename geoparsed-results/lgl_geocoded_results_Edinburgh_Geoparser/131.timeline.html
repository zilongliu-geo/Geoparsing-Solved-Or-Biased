Woman who stole thousands from charity given prison. A Columbia woman previously convicted of racking up hundreds of thousands of dollars’ worth of personal charges on her employer’s credit cards has been sentenced to two years, three months in prison for the crime. Wanda Y. Martin, 39, was convicted in federal court of wire fraud and was sentenced this week. In addition to the prison time, she must pay $342,991 back to Financial Services for Family Service Center, the U.S. Attorney’s office in Columbia said. Martin was the director of the nonprofit group when she used its cards to make personal purchases, then paid the bills from FSC accounts. FSC offers teen pregnancy support, children’s dentistry, consumer credit counseling and employment skills building.
