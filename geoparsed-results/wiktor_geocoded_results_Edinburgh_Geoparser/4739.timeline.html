Exeter is a town in Otsego County, New York, United States. The town is located in the northern part of the county. The population was 987 at the 2010 census. It is named after the county town and cathedral city of Exeter in England. The name Exeter derives from the city of Exeter in Devon, England. John Tunnicliff purchased 12,000 acres (49 km2) of a patent that included the town from David Schuyler and others in 1756, but had to abandon attempts to settle the town until after the French and Indian War. The first settlements were made around 1769 near Angel Hill. The town was formed from the Town of Richfield in 1799. Angel Hill – An elevation near the south town line. Brainard Corners – A hamlet west of Exeter hamlet on County Road 22 at the junction of County Road 19. Exeter (or Exeter Center) – A hamlet located near the center of the town on County Road 22. Exeter Corner – A location south of Exeter hamlet. Lidell Corners – A hamlet in the southeast corner of Exeter on NY-28 at County Road 19.
